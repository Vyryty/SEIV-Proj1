Career Insights

Computer science holds a massive variety of careers that you can choose from. Typically you will need some sort of formal education going into a career. In general it is also a field where you may need to constantly learn more. Technology today is rapidly developing and it is vital to stay up to date as well as growing you skills. Overall a career with computer science is hard work.

A career in computer science without going into the specialities be a beneficial journey. If you enjoy solving problems through coding or you aren't sure about what you want to do, straight computer science is the way to go. The positive things about having a career in computer science is that it is in high demand, especially for skilled individuals. This category of jobs is typically very important for creating and maintaining software in companies. There are also very many niche specialites you can invest into such as knowing COBOL and Fortran, because many old systems still need maintaince today. As a result of job variety, it tends to be one of the more beginner friendly sections with a wide range of job difficulty. The things that may be difficult about a career in computer science is that there typically is very high competition as a result of its commonality. Many jobs may prefer you to have 3-5 years of experience. Additionally, transitioning from a junior to a senior may prove difficult if you aren't continually learning.

  • Web developer/designer - $78,300 annual salary
  • Software developer - $109,020 annual salary
  • Computer and information research scientist - $102,600 annual salary

A career in cybersecurity is one of the more vital jobs today. Malicious actors are constantly creating new tech to exploit systems everyday. If you want to focus on computer safety and security, or are interested in activities related to hacking, this is the career for you. As a result of it being vital and required by law for some operations, it is a job in high demand. There are a good variety of jobs and as a result of learning about cybersecurity, you will gain in depth knowledge about how computers work. However, it is less beginner friendly than other sections because of the impact that a mistake may cause. It also almost certainly requires you to constantly learn in order to keep up with the technology. There tends to be a much higher standard to cybersecurity as you may need to know the laws and be very careful not to break them, or you'll need vigilance and an eye for detail. There is a great complexity to this field as well that may offput some people in addition to the rules and regulations that you must uphold.

Example entry jobs

  • Information security analyst - $90,595 annual salary
  • Information security specialist - $95,575 annual salary
  • Digital forensic examiner - $82,918 annual salary
  • IT auditor - $81,131 annual salary

A career in computer science with a business orientation possess an interesting set of jobs. Because it bridges a gap between the tech world and the business world, it is a career path high in demand. In turn, it additionally has a very wide range of jobs, one being data science, which is one of the most rapidly developing and in demand jobs today. Many of these jobs have a massive compensation. However many of these jobs require that you had a substantial amount of experience. Some jobs may require you to translate things to non tech-savvy people, which also demands for solid communication skills.

A career with games is the dream job for many, as many of us grew up with games. Because of its sheer popularity, it is very easy to get into even as a kid. There are hundreds of free resources online that range from block Java to full fledged Unity tutorials and free assets. Additionally in game development, your job may have a great variety of tasks within it, similar to software engineering. The downsides of having a career with games however is that projects are high risk. Attracting consumers had always been a difficult task and being in competition with the literal thousands of games being released every day doesn't help. There are many jobs available in the field and many have lax requirements, but in turn, jobs may be much less stable as a project may run out of budget or fracture as a result of conflict. Additionally, it's been reported that pay tends to be very low or is unstable when rushing to meet deadlines, and massive overtime is also incredibly common.
