Which Flavor of Computer Science?

You may have guessed, but computer science is actually a rather vague term. Computer science for the most part refers to just about any job that utilizes code to perform important functions on one or more computers. This can apply to several different jobs, anywhere from hundreds to even thousands of jobs, which may fall under computer science, but can be completely different in many ways. With this in mind, it can be very important to get an idea of what you want from your career in computer science, which can narrow down which field you'd like to go into. This page gives you some information to go on to gauge your interests, but if you'd like more information, click the link below!

Computer Science as a Career

If your specialty is just computer science as a whole, your options for careers will most likely be very flexible, ranging from web development to software engineering. If you don't have any particular focus with code, regular computer science can be a good route to take to find good jobs without limiting your options too much.

Computer Science with Gaming

Do you enjoy playing video games? Perhaps you'd like to make your own? Some people may think that video games are mostly an "art people" thing, but that's not really true! While artists are important for immersing an audience into a game, it's the programmers who actually make the mechanics and a large part of the functionality. Designing video games has a lot to do with programming and pseudo-science, since it's useful to know a thing or two to know how objects should interact with the world they exist in. If you wish you could create things in the real world with your coding skills, video games might just be what you're looking for!

Computer Science with Cyber Security

Whether it be a website, an application, video games, or just about anything else that has to do with a computer, everything needs protection. Computers are very volatile by design, which also means that some people can abuse that to steal information or damage computers. That's where cyber security comes in! There are several different kinds of cyber security jobs you could get, each with its own focus, but many of them have to do with defense against hackers. If you like puzzles or to find bugs in code, cyber security might be a good fit for you!

Computer Science with Business

While "computer science with business" may sound bland, it's a very broad and important distinction to make! Considering that computers are relatively new in human history, plenty of businesses are still working on digitizing and many people in those businesses are still trying to adapt to the evolving world of computers. A large part of this field would most likely be you using your computer expertise to help perform necessary company tasks, which could range from accounting to full-on software development! If you're not sure what specific way you'd like to apply your computer knowledge, but know that you want to be in a very professional environment, this may be the route for you!

Computer Science with Artificial Intelligence

In more recent years, artificial intelligence has become a hot commodity as computers are learning how to do increasingly more difficult tasks on their own. Though that is true, there is a lot of help behind the scenes for an AI to be able to do those things! In fact, there is enough specialized help that some companies just need help putting some together. The nice thing is that studying in artificial intelligence gives you a new skill that you could use just about anywhere, even if you don't understand the most advanced AI.

Data Science

One of the nice things about computers is that they live up to their name. They are really good at computing things, especially when those things are numbers! This is where data science comes into the picture as an important variant of computer science specialty. On the internet, companies who run websites can make money by gathering and selling data about their users which third parties can use to advertize products to them. That being said, they need a way to process, interpret, and understand the data that they collect, which is the primary job of a data scientist. If you enjoy transforming data into a human-readable format, data science may be right up your alley!
